## Purpose

Define mandatory procedures for responding to Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). This is a legal and ethical obligation with no exceptions.

## Definition

**CSAM (Child Sexual Abuse Material)**: Any visual depiction of sexually explicit conduct involving a minor (under 18 years old). This includes:
- Photographs
- Videos
- Digitally created images
- Modified images depicting minors

**Legal Framework:**
- U.S.: 18 U.S.C. ยง 2252, 2252A
- Reporting: NCMEC CyberTipline (mandatory)

## Mandatory Actions

### Immediate (Within 1 Hour)

1. **Suspend Account**
   - Immediately suspend user account
   - Block all platform access
   - Do NOT notify user

2. **Preserve Evidence**
   - Preserve all content (do not delete)
   - Capture metadata (IP, device, timestamps)
   - Screenshot user profile
   - Log all actions taken

3. **Report to NCMEC**
   - File CyberTipline report at [report.cybertip.org](https://report.cybertip.org)
   - Include all preserved evidence
   - Retain report confirmation number

4. **Internal Escalation**
   - Notify Trust & Safety Lead
   - Notify Legal
   - Notify Engineering Lead (for technical preservation)

### Within 24 Hours

1. **Legal Review**
   - Legal team reviews report
   - Determine law enforcement notification
   - Document decision

2. **Additional Preservation**
   - Full account data export
   - Communication history
   - Related account identifiers

### Ongoing

1. **Law Enforcement Cooperation**
   - Respond to legal process within deadlines
   - Preserve chain of custody
   - Coordinate with NCMEC

2. **User Account**
   - Permanent ban (no appeal)
   - Device fingerprint ban
   - Do not restore under any circumstances

## Evidence Handling

### Preservation Requirements

| Data Type | Retention | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| CSAM content | Do NOT store on our servers | Report to NCMEC, they preserve |
| Metadata | 90 days minimum | For law enforcement |
| User records | Permanent | Account, device, IP |
| Communications | 90 days minimum | Related to incident |

### Chain of Custody

1. Document who accessed evidence
2. Document when and why
3. Store in secure, access-controlled location
4. Never modify original evidence

## Prohibited Actions

**NEVER:**
- Share CSAM with anyone (including colleagues)
- Store CSAM on personal devices
- Delete CSAM before reporting
- Delay reporting for any reason
- Notify the user
- Discuss specifics outside need-to-know

## Staff Support

### Training
- All moderators trained on CSAM identification
- Annual refresher training required
- Psychological support resources available

### Well-being
- Exposure to CSAM is traumatic
- Mental health resources available
- Rotation policies to limit exposure
- Manager check-ins required

## Compliance

### Legal Requirements
- **Reporting**: Mandatory within 24 hours of discovery
- **Preservation**: Evidence must be preserved for law enforcement
- **Non-disclosure**: Do not alert user

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Federal criminal penalties
- Civil liability
- Platform integrity at risk
